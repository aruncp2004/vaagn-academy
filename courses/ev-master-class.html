What you'll learn?

Build your EV foundation. Master electrical fundamentals, battery systems, motors and controllers — the skills every EV industry job requires.

Concept Explanation

Clear, real-world EV theory from actual Vaagn manufacturing experience

5 Hands-on Practicals

Live lab sessions — vehicle walkaround, battery testing, diagnostics, performance evaluation and capstone project

Q&A with Faculty

Daily doubt clearing with EV engineers from Vaagn Auto

Week 1 (13–18 July)

EV Industry, Architecture & Fundamentals

Build your complete foundation — from the global EV ecosystem to reading OEM wiring diagrams on real vehicles.

5 lessons + 1 practical
  • L1EV Industry & Ecosystem — Global & Indian EV Market, OEM Structure
  • L2EV Architecture — Power Flow, HV & LV Systems
  • L3Electrical Fundamentals — Voltage, Current, Power, Circuits
  • L4OEM Wiring Diagrams — Schematics, Harness, Connectors
  • L5EV Components Deep Dive — Motor, Controller, Battery, VCU
  • P1Practical: Vehicle Walkaround & System Identification
Week 2 (20–25 July)

Battery Technology & Manufacturing

Deep dive into lithium battery chemistries, pack design, OEM manufacturing practices and failure analysis.

5 lessons + 1 practical
  • L6Lithium Battery Technologies — LFP, NMC, LTO, Cell Selection
  • L7Cell Engineering — Prismatic, Cylindrical & Pouch Cells
  • L8Battery Pack Design — Series-Parallel, Voltage & Capacity
  • L9OEM Battery Manufacturing — Cell Matching, IR Sorting, Assembly
  • L10Battery Failure Analysis — Swelling, Thermal Runaway, Ingress
  • P2Practical: OCV Testing, IR Testing, Cell Matching Workshop
Week 3 (27 July – 1 Aug)

BMS, Charging & Diagnostics

Master battery management systems, charging technologies, CAN communication and EV diagnostics methodology.

5 lessons + 1 practical
  • L11BMS Architecture — Protection Logic, SOC, SOH, Cell Balancing
  • L12BMS Configuration — Parameters, Fault Settings, Calibration
  • L13Charging Technologies — AC/DC Charging, Standards, CC-CV
  • L14CAN Communication — Architecture, Frames, Messages
  • L15EV Diagnostics — No Charge, No Drive, Low Range Conditions
  • P3Practical: CAN Data Reading, Fault Analysis, Charger Diagnostics
Week 4 (3–8 Aug)

Motors, Controllers & Performance

Understand EV propulsion systems, controller operation, vehicle performance engineering and drivetrain design.

5 lessons + 1 practical
  • L16Electric Motors — BLDC, PMSM, Induction Motors
  • L17Motor Controllers — PWM, Torque Control, Regen Braking
  • L18Vehicle Performance — Torque, Gradeability, Range Estimation
  • L19Drivetrain Engineering — Gear Ratios, Differential, Axles
  • L20Vehicle Validation & Testing Procedures
  • P4Practical: Current Measurements, Efficiency & Road Testing
Week 5 (10–15 Aug)

Manufacturing, Standards & Career

Learn OEM manufacturing systems, EV standards and certification, failure investigation and become fully job-ready.

5 lessons + 1 capstone
  • L21EV Manufacturing Systems — Production Flow, Quality Gates
  • L22Wiring Harness Engineering — Wire & Connector Selection
  • L23EV Standards — AIS-156, AIS-038, BIS, CMVR Overview
  • L24OEM Failure Investigation — Root Cause Analysis
  • L25Industry Readiness — Resume, Interview Prep, Career Pathways
  • P5Capstone: Battery Inspection, Vehicle Testing, Presentation
